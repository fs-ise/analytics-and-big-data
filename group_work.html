Group work

Summary

In this group project, you will develop and analyze a realistic business analytics case, including data, modeling, and critical reflection. While many analytics courses rely on publicly available datasets—often centered on societal or general-purpose questions—this project takes a different approach. We focus on business-relevant contexts such as management, finance, operations, or digital platforms that are more directly aligned with your future professional environments. To achieve this, you will design and work with synthetic datasets that reflect realistic organizational processes and decision situations. This allows you not only to apply analytical methods, but also to engage with the upstream challenge of framing meaningful problems and constructing data that captures them. Also: downstream challenge of deployment (e.g., ethics, …) Using suitable/reputable references to illustrate the prevalence/significance of the problem, and to show evidence that the proposed analytical approach and dataset is “typical” in practice

Objectives

  • Design and analyze realistic business data scenarios
  • Select and justify appropriate analytical methods
  • Communicate results to technical and non-technical audiences
  • Apply the CRISP-DM process as a scaffold
  • Reflect on limitations and improvements

Project Overview

Group Formation

  • The target group size is 4 students. We will select two groups with 5 students in a fair procedure. Groups with 5 students are expected to submit a more comprehensive case (+25%).
  • Once you know your group, create or join the group in the Canvas assignment.

Task

  • Select a topic and business question. Topics could include:

    • Finance (e.g., credit default, fraud, customer lifetime value)
    • Marketing (e.g., churn, customer segmentation, campaign effectiveness)
    • Human Resources (e.g., attrition, promotion, team productivity)
    • Operations (e.g., demand forecasting, inventory optimization, supplier risk scoring)
    • Digital business (e.g., user engagement prediction, gig worker retention, pricing strategy simulation)

  • Create a synthetic but realistic dataset to simulate internal company data. This could be complemented by synthetic or real external data.

  • Prepare an analytical notebook, following the CRISP-DM process:

    1. Business Understanding (What is the business context? What is the concrete question? Who are the stakeholders? Why does it matter?)
    2. Data Understanding (What does the dataset contain? How does it reflect a realistic business setting? What do you observe in the EDA?)
    3. Data Preparation (How is the data cleaned and transformed? What features are created? How is the data split?)
    4. Modeling (Which model is used? How is it trained? Why does it fit the problem?)
    5. Evaluation (Which metrics are used? How well does the model perform? What do the results mean in business terms?)
    6. Deployment (How would the model be used in practice? What inputs/outputs are required? What are key limitations or risks?)

  • Develop a report to reflect on the case (what students will learn/specific challenge; how it illustrates and complements materials covered in the lecture; what its simplyfying assumptions or limitations are; how it extends existing work; potential for improvement)

NoteNotes

  • Support key elements of your analysis with reputable sources (industry reports or academic literature), especially for:

    • problem relevance
    • typical data structures
    • common modeling approaches

  • Simplification is acceptable. If a problem would typically require more advanced methods (e.g., reinforcement learning), you may use a simpler, well-justified approach.

  • Optional extensions (for stronger projects) may include:

    • advanced data preparation or feature engineering
    • model comparison or refinement
    • robustness checks or extended evaluation
    • more detailed deployment considerations

Evaluation Criteria

A max of 60 points can be earned, adding to the final grade. Each group receives one grade.

Category Points Criteria
A. Problem and case design 15 pts Relevance and clarity of business problem; Realism and quality of dataset design
B. Methodological rigor 20 pts Proper use of CRISP-DM; Justification of methods; Correct implementation
C. Analysis quality 15 pts Insightfulness of results and interpretation
D. Communication 5 pts Clarity, structure, visualizations
E. Code quality 5 pts Clean, documented, runnable

Note: The applied methodology is more important for the evaluation than the absolute model performance.

Synthetic Data Requirement

  • Must reflect realistic business data

  • Could include noise, missing values, duplicates

  • Must be documented in the script:

    • How it was generated
    • Assumptions (ideally with evidence)

Difficulty Levels

  • Standard: basic pipeline + one model
  • Advanced: focused extension in individual areas, such as specific data preparation challenge, interactive visualization, feature engineering, model refinement, advanced evaluation

AI policy

  • Allowed for debugging, code explanations, documentation support
  • Not allowed to generate solutions
  • Students must explain and defend their work if needed. Everything must be validated.
