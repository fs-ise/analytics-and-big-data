Group work

In this group project, you will develop and analyze a realistic business analytics case, including data, modeling, and critical reflection. While many analytics courses rely on publicly available datasets—often centered on societal or general-purpose questions—this project takes a different approach. We focus on business-relevant contexts such as management, finance, operations, or digital platforms that are more directly aligned with your future professional environments. To achieve this, you will design and work with synthetic datasets that reflect realistic organizational processes and decision situations. This allows you not only to apply analytical methods, but also to engage with the upstream challenge of framing meaningful problems and constructing data that captures them.

1. Learning Objective

  • Apply the CRISP-DM process end-to-end
  • Design and analyze realistic business data scenarios
  • Select and justify appropriate analytical methods
  • Communicate results to technical and non-technical audiences
  • Reflect on limitations and improvements

Topic suggestions

  • Human Resources (e.g., attrition, promotion, team productivity)
  • Finance (e.g., credit default, fraud, customer lifetime value)

  • Operations (e.g., demand forecasting, inventory optimization, supplier risk scoring)

Group Size and Application

  • You can form your own groups. The target group size is 4 students.
  • If you have formed a group, please send me an email with the names and email addresses of all group members.
  • Groups of 4 will be automatically accepted.
  • If you are not yet in a group, you can still participate: the allocation of remaining students will take place during a session (not after the deadline).
  • If the total number of students does not divide evenly into groups of 4, some groups may have 3 or 5 members, determined in a fair and transparent way.
